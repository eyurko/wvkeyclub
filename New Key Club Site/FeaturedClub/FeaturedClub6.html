Shady Spring High School

The Shady Spring Key Club!

Shady Spring High School's Key Club is full of determined and helpful members who strive to make a difference in the community.

During this past year, SSHS Key Club has brought about projects that involve the entire school. A PINK OUT Football Game will be held every year in October to help the fight against cancer. The Annual Food Drive had made its fourth appearance at Shady Spring High School. This year the Key Clubbers and the rest of the SSHS student body had brought in over 3,500 canned items to help a local food pantry. There was also a Food and Hygiene Drive to support the Ronald McDonald House Charity, along with a collection of pop tabs.

At the moment, Key Club members are ready to spread the word of the Key Club experience to upcoming SSHS Freshman. A committee has been formed to provide information on Key Club and preferred charities so that the younger teens will be fully prepared for the excitement of Key Club their following year.

SSHS Key Club has also had a lot of fun this year with the local Beckley Kiwanians.

Early in the year, Key Club helped with The Kiwanian Pancake Day in Beckley to help serve a breakfast to the community. SSHS Key Club also helped out at Tailgate Halloween to distribute candy to local trick-or-treaters, as well as selling drinks at Beckley's Chili Night to support the Eliminate Project. The Kiwanis Christmas Party in December, along with ringing the bell for the Salvation Army, really brought Key Clubbers closer to the spirit of volunteering and fun. Members of the Key Club at Shady Spring High School are also excited for the upcoming picnic and show with the Kiwanians at Grandview State Park.

Throughout the summer, Key Club plans to remain involved with the community. On July 12th, Key Club members plan to attend Relay for Life at the Raleigh County Crossroads Mall and raise money for the battle against cancer. A Planning Retreat for the members has also been scheduled, so that they members are able to bond and learn the ideals of Key Club and volunteering.
