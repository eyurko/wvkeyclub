Oak Glen High School

The Oak Glen Key Club!

With a new adviser, Morgan Martin, the Oak Glen High School Key Club spent the 2012-13 school year rebuilding its foundation. They increased their membership and met more frequently. At the end of the year, officers were elected. President Megan Nally, Vice President Cullen Bennett, Secretary Maddy Hill, Treasurer Natelie Chappell, Editor Tre Beaumont, and District Project Chair Abby Mercer have big plans to continue to build upon the foundation established last year.

Going into the 2013-14 school year, OGHS Key Club has thirty returning members. They spent the first two weeks of school conducting a membership drive, which has garnered interest from at least thirty additional students. From this outlook, the club should almost double in size. The officers have created a sense of excitement with posters and announcements for the first official meeting of the year, which took place on Thursday, September 12.

The club is looking forward to continuing some of the traditions of the past. The first big event of the school year will be the Sadie Hawkins Dance. A portion of the funds raised from the dance will be donated to a charitable cause to be determined at a later date. The club also plans to continue to volunteer at the local soup kitchen, a service activity that began last year. Every Wednesday, Key Clubbers will report to the Weirton United Methodist Church to serve food and socialize with community members.

In addition to continuing tradition, the group also plans to create new traditions. Goals for the year include increasing membership and participation, raising more funds specifically for the district project and major emphasis, and organizing more club activities and service opportunities. Oak Glen High School's Key Club is committed to service for its school and community. Students recognize the importance of the impact that they can have.
