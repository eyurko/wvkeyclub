What Are "Dues"?

Dues are a small fee paid by Key Club members in order to belong to Key Club International. In the West Virginia District, dues are typically at least $13.00, with the clubs adding their own dues on to the price. Dues are divided up as follows:

$6.50 goes to Key Club International.
$6.50 goes to the West Virginia District.
An extra fee can be assesed by the club.

Why Do We Have to Pay Dues?

Like any organization, Key Club needs money to function at all levels. Both the District and International levels need money for their board meetings and conventions to help promote the core values in our future leaders as well as expose them to leadership opportunities from a young age. Also, many districts also use the money to provide scholarships to qualifying members, such as the Thomas Bennett Scholarship the District Scholarship. International also uses the money to create and distribute materials such as various promotional materials, posters, and the Key Club magazine through the Key Club Website. Similarly, the West Virginia District uses dues to distribute materials and maintain their own individual website. Furthermore, many clubs add on their own dues to build and maintain a club treasury which may be used for a variety of things--officer retreats, convention scholarships, and funding for various projects. As a member, your dues help make Key Club great at every level!

What Are Our Club Dues?

For more information pertaining to how your club dues are specifically used, please see your faculty advisor, as those dues are set by the club and likely detailed in your club's bylaws.

Important Dates

October 1st – Clubs may start the dues process in the Membership Update Center November 1st – Early Bird Dues deadline 
December 1st – On time Dues deadline; Non-paying clubs become delinquent 
December 31st – First time late notice to Key Club President and Advisors 
January 15th – Faculty and Kiwanis Advisors of delinquent clubs emailed about delinquent notice to school principals 
January 31st – Second late notice to Sponsoring Kiwanis February 1st – Clubs who have not paid dues become suspended 
February 15th – School principal sent notification of delinquent dues status 
October 1st – Clubs who have not paid dues become inactive, and if they pay dues in the next year they must pay a $100 reactivation fee.

And If We Don't Pay Dues?

If you personally do not pay dues, you are not a member "in good standing" with Key Club International. As such, you cannot become an officer at any level--Club, District, or International, you cannot vote at your District's House of Delegates, and you cannot attend any conventions. Furthermore, you are rendered ineligible for any District or International scholarship opportunities.

Should your club fail to submit its dues to Key Club International in a timely fashion (see above dates), it could become suspended or inactive (and require a reactivation fee to become active again) as well as become ineligible for any awards, honors, or grants such as a Youth Opportunities Fund grant. After a long enough period of time, the Club can actually lose its charter and have to start the long process of rechartering once again.
