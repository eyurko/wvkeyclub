What Does This Even Mean?

If your club becomes inactive or suspended, it means that your club failed to submit dues in a timely fashion. See the dues page for the dates that your club needs to turn in their dues by.

What If We're Not Sure?

If you do not know if your club has paid its dues or not, please contact the District Administrator or the Lieutenant Governor for your division--they have or can quickly get a list of which clubs have paid dues, and they will be happy to help you sort out the confusion.

So We're Suspended, Now What?

To get your club off the suspended list, simply pay your dues by October 1st of the current year. Your club will be reactivated and can continue as usual.

...And If We're Inactive?

If your club is inactive, in addition to your dues for the year your club must also pay a $100 reactivation fee. Understandably, it is better to be suspended than inactive (though we'd prefer that you were neither). However, aside from the reactviation fee's added cost, reactivation is a simple procedure and you will quickly be able to get your club back on its feet.

At Least That's As Bad As It Gets, Right?

Unfortunately, no. Too long of a delay and your club can get its charter revoked, meaning that it is no longer a Key Club and cannot refer to itself as such. If this occurs, you will probably need to consult the "How Do I Start a Key Club" page, as you will need to get the club rechartered and effectively start building the club from scratch. This is NOT something you want to happen to your club, so remember to pay your dues!
