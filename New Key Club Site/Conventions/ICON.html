As every well-versed Key Clubber knows, Key Club International is the world’s largest and oldest student-led service organization.  With more than 260,000 teen-aged members from all over the world, it’s easy to see why we are the largest organization.  So where does the world’s largest service organization meet?  Why, Key Club International Convention, of course!

International Convention is a semi-formal convention with a focus on district-level communication.  This multi-cultural convention is in a different U.S. city every year, and it takes place in a large hotel or combination of hotels and convention centers. This year it is July 1-8, 2013, in Washington, D.C. It's so close by!  Key Club members from all thirty countries are invited to come for a week of fun-filled activities and service opportunities.  Typically, there is a service walk at International Convention, along with several service projects and many informational forums that provide all attendees with ideas and inspiration to improve their home clubs.  The ‘big deal’ about International Convention is being able to elect the new International Board of Trustees and see the retirement of the immediate past International Board on the last night of convention.

At International Convention, Key Clubbers have the rare opportunity to interact with teenagers of different cultural backgrounds who share the common goal of improving the world. Key Clubbers can also bond with club members from their own district during the district tours; no matter what city International Convention is in, there is always the guarantee of historical monuments, family fun centers, and many other unforgettable adventures.  Over the course of one week, Key Clubbers can make innumerable friendships that will last a lifetime.  Your week at International Convention is certain to be filled with the perfect combination of service, leadership, learning, and excitement.  

So watch for the announcements about this year’s International Convention! You don’t want to miss it!
