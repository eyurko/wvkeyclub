District Convention is a three-day annual convention held in Charleston with the purpose of educating club members, training club officers, and electing a new District Board of Trustees.  There are informal events, such as the Key Club dance, and formal events, such as forums and caucusing.  Over these three days, individuals are praised for dedication, clubs are recognized for achievements, and delegates are elected into office.  There is free time, entertainment, motivational speakers, and stories about the famous ‘Key Club Moment’s.

District Convention is the ultimate definition of Key Club, with its informal and fun-loving attitude mixed with the formal atmosphere of responsibility.  And who ever said we couldn’t have both?  You will get the best of all things Key Club related at District Convention.  Club members can attend informational forums to learn about Key Club and swap ideas with clubs from other divisions.  Club officers and interested club members can attend forums to be trained in their respective positions and learn new organization skills and management tips.

One of the most important parts of District Convention is caucusing, voting, and installing the new District Board of Trustees.  Students just like you and I run for offices on the board, make speeches, and answer your questions.  Then each school will cast votes for candidates in the position of Lieutenant Governor, Secretary, Governor, and several others.  On the last day, the new board is installed, and a fresh set of faces are ready to take on the challenges of leading a new Key Club year.  Awards are given for everything from service hours to best poster to best club video.  There are a hundred ways to win an award, so watch for packets from our District Administrator if you want to enter your club in a contest!

Details on this year's District Convention are still up in the air, so keep an eye out here and our Facebook and Twitter for more information!
